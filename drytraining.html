Dry training

Dry training for freediving is a fundamental aspect of preparing the body and mind for the challenges that await beneath the waves. This specialized training regimen focuses on honing key skills and physical attributes essential for freediving, all conducted outside of the water. Dry training encompasses a range of exercises, including breath-holding techniques, stretching routines, and strength conditioning exercises that target the specific muscle groups involved in freediving. Breath-holding exercises help participants improve lung capacity and develop better breath control, crucial for extended dives.


Stretching routines enhance flexibility, promoting better body positioning and reducing the risk of injury during underwater descents. Strength conditioning exercises, particularly focusing on the core and leg muscles, contribute to improved buoyancy control and overall physical fitness. Through a structured and comprehensive dry training program, freedivers can enhance their performance, increase their breath-holding capabilities, and cultivate the physical and mental resilience needed for a safe and rewarding underwater experience.

Cardiovascular fitness plays a crucial role in breathing and apnea (breath-holding) abilities for several reasons. Engaging in regular cardiovascular exercise, such as running, cycling, or swimming, enhances the efficiency of the respiratory system and contributes to overall respiratory health. Here's why cardio is important for breathing and apnea.

  1. Increased Lung Capacity: Cardiovascular exercises elevate the demand for oxygen, leading to improved lung capacity over time. This increased capacity allows individuals to take in more air during each breath, a vital factor in breath-holding activities like apnea.

  2. Improved Oxygen Utilization: Cardio training enhances the body's ability to efficiently use oxygen. This is essential for freedivers, as they rely on stored oxygen during apnea dives. Improved oxygen utilization allows for more effective breath-holding and better performance in underwater activities.

  3. Enhanced Respiratory Muscles: Cardio exercises strengthen the respiratory muscles, including the diaphragm and intercostal muscles. Stronger respiratory muscles make breathing more controlled and efficient, supporting the demands of breath-holding and prolonged apnea.

  4. Reduced Resting Heart Rate: Regular cardio workouts contribute to a lower resting heart rate. A lower resting heart rate indicates that the heart is working more efficiently, pumping a greater volume of blood with each beat. This efficiency is beneficial during breath-holding activities, as the body can distribute oxygen more effectively.

  5. Improved Endurance: Cardiovascular fitness increases overall endurance, allowing individuals to sustain physical activities for longer durations. This endurance is beneficial for freedivers, helping them withstand extended breath-holding sessions and navigate the challenges of underwater exploration.

  • Incorporating cardiovascular exercises into a training routine not only enhances general fitness but also directly contributes to the respiratory adaptations necessary for successful breath-holding and apnea experiences.
